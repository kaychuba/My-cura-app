# My-Cura Privacy Policy

> **Status: DRAFT for legal review** — publish on the website and inside the
> apps before onboarding real users. Plain-English by design.

*Last updated: 19 July 2026*

## Who we are

My-Cura Ltd *(confirm entity)* provides care-management software to UK care
agencies. **For the care records inside the platform, your care agency is
the data controller and My-Cura is their data processor** — the agency
decides what is recorded; we keep it safe and available. For your account
with us (login, support), My-Cura is the controller.

## What we process

- **Service users:** name, date of birth, address, health conditions, care
  plans, visit notes, medication records, consent decisions, emergency and
  clinical contacts.
- **Care workers and office staff:** name, contact details, employment and
  training records, payroll data, the location of your phone **at the
  moment you clock in or out of a shift** (never continuous tracking).
- **Everyone:** login details (passwords stored only as strong one-way
  hashes), security events such as login attempts and IP addresses.

## Why (lawful bases)

Care records: your agency's legal obligations as a regulated care provider,
and the provision of health and social care (UK GDPR art. 9(2)(h)).
Optional matters such as photography use recorded consent, which can be
withdrawn at any time and withdrawal is kept in the history. Platform
security and improvement: legitimate interests.

## How we protect it

Every agency's data is isolated at the database level; administrators must
use multi-factor authentication; sensitive fields and all backups are
encrypted; every change to a care record is written to an audit trail that
cannot be edited or deleted, even by us.

## How long we keep it

Care records: 8 years after care ends (NHS records code). Payroll: 6 years
plus current (HMRC). Audit logs: 3 years. Backups: 30 days. Full schedule:
available on request.

## Your rights

Access, rectification, erasure (subject to statutory care-record retention,
where we must record a refusal reason), restriction, portability and
objection. Contact your care agency first (the controller); we support them
in fulfilling every request. Complaints: the ICO (ico.org.uk).
